Savory / Pretzel Buns

Cooking or cookery is the art of preparing food for consumption with the use of heat. Cooking techniques and ingredients vary widely across the world, reflecting unique environmental, economic, and cultural traditions and trends. The way that cooking takes place also depends on the skill and type of training an individual cook has. Cooking can also occur through chemical reactions without the presence of heat, most notably with Ceviche, a traditional South American dish where fish is cooked with the acids in lemon or lime juice. Sushi also uses a similar chemical reaction between fish and the acidic content of rice glazed with vinegar.

Preparing food with heat or fire is an activity unique to humans, and some scientists believe the advent of cooking played an important role in human evolution. Most anthropologists believe that cooking fires first developed around 250,000 years ago. The expansion of agriculture, commerce, trade and transportation between civilizations in different regions offered cooks many new ingredients.

Pretzel Bun Recipe: (8-10 servings)

Dough Ingredients:
  • 1 c warm water
  • 3/4 c warm milk
  • 2 T unsalted butter
  • 3/4 tsp salt
  • 4 1/2 c all purpose flour
  • 1 packet instant yeast
  • Course salt for topping
Water Bath Ingredients:
  • 2 quarts water
  • 1 T salt
  • 1/4 c baking soda
Directions:
  1. Mix together the dry dough ingredients. Stir in the milk, water, and butter. Kneed by hand or in stand mixer until dough is smooth and slightly sticky.
  2. Place dough in a lightly greased bowl, cover, and allow to rise in a warm place until doubled, about an hour.
  3. Gently deflate the dough and divide into 8-10 pieces. Form each piece into a ball and place on a lightly greased baking sheet. Left rise 15 minutes.
  4. While the dough is rising, preheat oven to 400 F. In a large pot, bring the water bath ingredients to a boil.
  5. Once the dough has risen, place 4 or 5 of the dough balls into the boiling water bath. Boil 30 seconds on one side, then flip and boil for 30 seconds on the other. Remove the dough balls from the bath back to the baking sheet and repeat with the remaining dough.
  6. Use a sharp knife or razor blade to make slashes 1/2 inch deep in the top of the buns. Sprinkle with coarse salt.
  7. Bake the buns for 20-24 minutes until dark brown.
