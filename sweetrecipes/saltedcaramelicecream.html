Sweet / Salted Caramel Ice Cream

Cooking or cookery is the art of preparing food for consumption with the use of heat. Cooking techniques and ingredients vary widely across the world, reflecting unique environmental, economic, and cultural traditions and trends. The way that cooking takes place also depends on the skill and type of training an individual cook has. Cooking can also occur through chemical reactions without the presence of heat, most notably with Ceviche, a traditional South American dish where fish is cooked with the acids in lemon or lime juice. Sushi also uses a similar chemical reaction between fish and the acidic content of rice glazed with vinegar.

Preparing food with heat or fire is an activity unique to humans, and some scientists believe the advent of cooking played an important role in human evolution. Most anthropologists believe that cooking fires first developed around 250,000 years ago. Salted Caramel Ice Cream Recipe:

Ingredients:
  • 1 c. + 2 T sugar, divided
  • 2 c. heavy cream, room temperature
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 1 c. whole milk
  • 1/2 tsp salt
Directions:
  1. Have ingredients ready and close at hand, once caramel starts to cook, it can cook very quickly and burn easily. Put 2 tablespoon sugar in a heavy bottom pot. Heat over medium high heat.
  2. Once the sugar has melted and starts to turn golden, stir in an additional 1/2 cup of sugar, two tablespoons at a time. Once sugar is a deep amber color, quickly turn off heat and add cream to pot to stop cooking. Caramel might steam and splatter a bit, so be careful. If any of the caramel crystalizes, heat back up gently and stir until dissolved.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together egg yolks and remaining half cup of sugar until well combined and light yellow.
  4. Add milk and salt to caramel mixture and heat over medium heat until just barely boiling.
  5. Slowly whisk about half a cup of milk mixture into the egg yolks to temper the eggs and warm them up. Then add yolks to remainder of the milk and heat over medium heat until mixture thickens and reaches 165 degrees.
  6. Strain mixture into a bowl and chill in the fridge at least 4 hours or overnight. Once chilled, freeze in ice cream maker according to manufacturer's instructions.
